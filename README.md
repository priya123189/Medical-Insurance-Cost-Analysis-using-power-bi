📊 Medical Insurance Cost Analysis – Power BI Project
🔍 Project Overview

This Power BI project analyzes medical insurance costs using the Medical Cost Personal Dataset. 
The goal of this project is to identify key cost drivers, understand customer demographics, and evaluate the impact of health and lifestyle factors such as BMI and smoking status on insurance charges.
This project is designed as a portfolio project for an entry-level Data Analyst role.

📁 Dataset Information
Dataset Name: Medical Cost Personal Dataset
Source: Kaggle
Rows: 1,338
Columns: 7

Columns Description:
age – Age of the insured person,
age_group – Age category of the insured person
Under 25
25 – 45
Above 45,
sex – Gender (male/female),
bmi – Body Mass Index (decimal numeric value),
children – Number of dependents,
smoker – Smoking status (yes/no),
region – Residential region,
charges – Medical insurance cost,

📄 Report Pages
Page 1: Overall Insurance Overview
Total Insurance Charges.
Average Charges per Customer.
Total Customers.
Average BMI.
Smoker vs Non-Smoker Cost Comparison.
Regional Cost Comparison.
BMI Distribution Analysis.
Key Insight:
Smokers contribute significantly higher insurance costs compared to non-smokers across all regions.

Page 2: Customer Demographic Analysis
Average Insurance Charges by Age Group.
Gender Distribution.
Children vs Average Insurance Charges.
Regional & Gender-based filtering using slicers.
Key Insight:
Middle-aged customers (25–45) and customers with no or fewer dependents show higher average insurance charges.

Page 3: Risk & Cost Driver Analysis
Relationship between BMI and Insurance Charges.
Impact of Smoking Status on Total Charges.
Regional Risk Analysis by Smoking Status.
Detailed summary table with average and total charges.
Key Insight:
Higher BMI combined with smoking status results in the highest insurance costs, making them key risk factors.
